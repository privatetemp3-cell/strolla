The Experience

A relaxed Cape Town meeting place, just off Beach Road.

Start with coffee after a promenade walk, settle in for brunch, stay for sushi, or come back later for cocktails on the deck. Strolla is the kind of place that works at any hour — unhurried but never dull.

Alongside the Peninsula All-Suite Hotel, with the Atlantic within sight, there's a reason locals keep coming back and visitors make it their first stop. The menu spans from honest breakfast plates to proper sushi, craft cocktails and a selection of wines that earn their place on any good afternoon.

For Tourists & Brunch Lovers

Made for slow mornings, promenade walks & Cape Town afternoons.

If you're staying nearby in Sea Point, Bantry Bay or Green Point, Strolla is the obvious answer to "where should we eat?" Walk the promenade, work up an appetite, and settle in for a proper spread.

The deck catches the Atlantic breeze. The coffee is good. The brunch menu has enough range to keep everyone at the table happy — from a simple egg & toast to a full English or a loaded omelette.

After brunch, you're a short stroll from the beach, the tidal pools and the promenade. Come back for sushi in the afternoon and cocktails as the sun dips west. That's a good Cape Town day.

Drinks & Sundowners

Sea Point mornings. Sushi afternoons. Cocktails after dark.

The bar at Strolla earns its place. A considered list of cocktails, a well-stocked spirits selection, South African wines, craft beers and local ciders — served at a pace that suits where you are.

Whether it's an Aperol Spritz on arrival, a house Strolla Fizz alongside sushi on the deck, or a Johnnie Walker Sunset Derby as the light changes over the Atlantic, the drinks here are grown-up without being pretentious.

Family-Friendly

The grown-up restaurant that families also love.

Strolla is not a children's restaurant — but it is unambiguously family-friendly. The kids' menu is considered, the play area keeps little ones engaged, and the deck & dining room hold their own as a proper restaurant and bar experience for the adults.

It's the kind of place where parents can have a real lunch while the children are entertained, rather than rushing through a meal. Good food, a relaxed atmosphere and a table worth sitting at for a while.

Kids under 10 welcome in the play area. Parents required to sign an indemnity.
